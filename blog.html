Artificial Intelligence: The Future Of Work

Over the past decade, technology has advanced and become a force to be reckoned with. Specifically, in the area of work, a myriad of processes have been optimized to ensure speed and accuracy in data gathering/ analysis, automation of office processes, and so much more. Yet, a new era of technological advancement is here and its promises are known to be so disruptive, it might completely change the nature of work as we know it. With Artificial Intelligence (AI), a wide range of tasks that have been stunted by human limitation through emotional influences and error-prone analysis can now be performed by machines. The difference in results is known to be exponential; a simple example is how a machine can translate over a thousand languages in mere seconds – a task no group of humans will ever be able to carry out accurately.

This same astronomic advancement is the future of work. Automation provides an opportunity for companies to attain heights never before reached in many industries. The most apparent ones are those by tech giants using tools of speech recognition for Amazon’s Alexa, the highly AI-driven smartphones at our disposal, autopilot hybrid cars, and more. Yet, the future of AI goes beyond these. In the construction industry, AI is already being used to optimize assembling lines, predict equipment maintenance and to solidify procedures. The British DataSparQ company also announced the release of the "A.I. Bar" which places individuals who come up to the bar in a line using cameras and sensors as a means of swift facial recognition.

Mass markets like the foodservice industry, through AI, have made it possible to order food on demand and more opportunities abound to streamline processes for their potential clients. It is no surprise why the fast-food chain, McDonald, has embraced digital technology even to the point of purchasing two (2) AI firms with the aim of launching and speeding up the growth of what it calls its Future Restaurant Experience. The potential benefits to these industries are so profound that McKinsey forecasts that AI and machine learning could create an additional $2.6 trillion in value by 2020 in Marketing and Sales, and up to $2 trillion in manufacturing and supply chain planning.

In all these things, an apparent risk exists: Will AI displace workers as they lose their jobs to automated processes in the not so distant future? Probably. This risk is imminent with many job functions being replaced on a daily basis by computers and advanced technologies. However, research has proven otherwise. A report by Gartner explains that Artificial Intelligence will create more jobs than it eliminates, forecasting that year 2020 alone will experience the creation of 2.3 million jobs while eliminating 1.8 million which is a net gain of 500,000 newly created jobs. Forrester also estimated that automation could result in a net worth loss of around 7% of jobs in the US by 2027, replacing 17% of jobs although creating new jobs comparable to 10% of the workforce.

This narrative puts forward the ideology that even automated jobs require a level of human skill and experience. There will be a shift from conventional, traditional, and less accurate processes to fully automated systems and this in itself, provides an opportunity for the business owner who wants to be relevant in the world of tomorrow.

Opportunities to be Leveraged

The technology of the future provides maximum convenience, faster processes, fully integrated control systems, faster communication, access to better information, and so on. The best part is that no aspect of business processes will be left unturned. Modern businesses will require more automated processes and consciousness of the exponential advancement of the times. While some sectors might indeed fold, new sectors will emerge and it will be up to the businesses of today to position themselves where the more technologically-savvy individuals of tomorrow will demand their services. As Andrew Yang put it, “The impending destruction of jobs due to automation and AI technologies is definitely increasing the need for – and speed at which – we have to implement big solutions, such as a universal basic income.” Investment in AI and work process automation increases productivity and gives businesses a bigger canvass to paint big ideas on.

An example of this futuristic era of tech is what is already being put in place by vScheduler which provides effortless guest profiling as opposed to the slower methods of manual recording and tracking, better seamless front desk management for clients, automation of client services and so much more. This new wave of technology has something for everybody and it promises a world where speed, accuracy, ease of information, high performance and so on, become the new normal.

With the opportunities that abound, there’s no narrative in which the world will opt for its current processes over a better life with high-tech systems. Research has shown that the technological developments expected over the next five (5) decades, just like job creation, will leave many amazing net positive impacts on society at large and transform the global village into a global smart city.

Businesses now have a huge opportunity to improve work productivity and reduce the quantity of direct labor, thus placing a higher value on specific tech-based skills above others. The limitations of humans like emotions can now be shoved aside as technology takes the front burner, ensuring a rise in productivity and efficiency. Researchers like Ristan Harris, an expert on how software is getting rid of our emotional limitations, have taken the leap to determine how they can make the most out of the opportunity that is AI and you should too.

As Jeff Bezos puts it, “What’s dangerous is not to evolve, not to invent, and not to improve the customer experience.” The move to AI is undoubtedly one of the biggest opportunities in the future of work and there is no better time than now to make the move.
